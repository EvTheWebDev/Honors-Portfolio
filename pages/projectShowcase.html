Project Showcase: JavaScript Lesson Site

For my Examining Self As Teacher class, we were tasked with creating a lesson to teach a concept of our choice. I chose to create a site that teaches the concept of recursion, or looping, in JavaScript.

As a part of my honors contract, I created a website for this lesson, and presented it to my class.

This project was a great opportunity for me to apply my JavaScript skills in a practical way and to create something that could be useful for others learning the language.

What I Learned

The Code:

This project was a great opportunity for me to apply my JavaScript skills in a practical way and to create something that could be useful for others learning the language. Here are some things I got practice with:

  • DOM Manipulation
    • Changing HTML content and styling with JavaScript
  • Event Listeners
    • Listening for user interactions and responding to them
  • Functions and Recursion
    • For Loops, While Loops, and Do While Loops

The Presentation

This project also gave me the opportunity to practice my presentation skills. I had to present my lesson to my class, which was a great way to practice explaining complex concepts in a clear and concise way.

None of the other students in my education class had coding experience, so I had to give them a baseline on computer science before I could start the lesson.

Teaching to an audience with no experience was a very challenging task, but it was a good representation of how a introductory computer science course for high schoolers would be. It was very important for me to realize just how much of my knowledge was built through my education, and through my experience with technology before college started.

On top of that, I needed to practice live demonstrations of my code. Computer Science professors always dread the "live demo", and now I understand why. Everything that could go wrong did go wrong. This almost turns the lecture into improv, as I attempt to solve issues on the spot while maintaining composure in front of the audience.

To Reflect

Looking back on this project, I realize that this was the moment that made me want to teach computer science. I had been interested in education enough to take the course, but actually seeing those "Aha!" moments in my classmates was a powerful realization of the ways I could help others.

The site itself may not be that pretty, but it represents an amazing opportunity for me to find my passion in education and computer science.
