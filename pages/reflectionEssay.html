Retrospective Reflection: Compiling the Future of Tech Education

Bridging the Gap Between Code and Comprehension

When I first entered the Luddy School of Informatics at IU(PU)I, my focus was mainly on web development and video game design. However, as it stands now, I've realized that my true passion doesn't just lie in building software, it lies in teaching others how to build it. My past year as an Honors Scholar has been defined by my pivot toward education, which ultimately led me to enroll in the Master of Arts for Teachers (MAT) program through IU Online.

As one of the first students to go through the program without a traditional education background, I have had to blaze my own trail in my coursework. By doing this, I hope to prove that Luddy students can successfully pursue education as a graduate student, without needing an undergrad education degree as well.

Constructing Knowledge: The JavaScript Lesson Website

One of my first attempts at a computer science lesson happened during the Examining Self as Teacher course in the Fall of my Junior year, where I designed and developed the JavaScript Lesson Website hosted on my professional portfolio. This was a whole year before I was even aware of the MAT program.

Building this site (and the lesson associated with it) required me to take complex programming paradigms from my Luddy coursework and break them down into accessible, interactive pieces. I had to anticipate where beginners might get stuck and design the demos to gently guide them toward their own understanding, rather than just handing them the answers. On top of that, I had to give this lesson to my peers, even though they had ZERO knowledge of web development.

Goal Alignment: My development of the interactive JavaScript Lesson Website on my portfolio is evidence that my work achieves the IUI Honors College goal to develop strong communication, problem-solving, and civic-minded skills.

Trailblazing in the MAT Program and Campus Leadership

My passion for guiding others didn't just appear out of nowhere; its roots trace back to when I was a Boy Scout leading my troop. Then, it popped up again during my first summer in college, when I became a camp counselor. However, formalizing that passion through the MAT courses I began last Spring required a massive leap. I had to rapidly adapt to pedagogical theories and classroom management strategies in classes like Assessment In Schools or Instruction in the Context of Curriculum.

This pivot to helping others was made significantly easier by my on campus jobs. Serving as a Resident Assistant and a Peer Advisor allowed me to put instruction into daily practice. Whether I was mediating a conflict or advising a first-year student on their schedule, I was actively developing my teaching voice.

Goal Alignment: My trailblazing integration of MAT coursework with my hands-on campus leadership roles as an RA and Peer Advisor aligns with the IUI Honors College goal to participate in engaged learning experiences and applied learning.

Emerging Expertise: Taking Tech Education Abroad

As I prepare to graduate, my Honors and MAT experiences have perfectly aligned with my immediate professional goals: teaching abroad in Japan through the JET Program. While I still have the passion for web development that brought me into Luddy in the first place, I am very excited to see where my new love for education will take me.

The Honors College provided the framework for me to forge this unique path, fundamentally changing my perspective on what a career in technology could look like. Whether or not I go to Japan, I know that I want to teach. I never would have realized that I was capable of actually pursing education seriously if it weren't for my time experimenting with my Honors Contract in my education course.
